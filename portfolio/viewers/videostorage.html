Project Title

Caption of the game.

I decided to set up a simple website dedicated to my recordings in regards to logs, self-reference tutorials, and stream archives. The place stores videos and documents for reading more tips. There includes bundled playlists and project workings. I created this as a means of sharing my content and allowing for discussions for viewers if they felt the need. The design result turned out to quite impressive to my knowledge. More on about page.

Tools used.

  • HTML: Design, Layout
  • CSS: Styles, colouring, backdrop.
  • Javascript: Events, dynamic pages
  • jQuery: Events
  • Wamp: Server testing, database creation.
  • Three JS: Interactive games on display.

The initialisation went well. The categorisation had a bit of trouble. I had referenced the W3C pages to look at the structuring and styling. Created a few blank instances of the blocks to visualise how it would end up at upon publishing data. I decided to sort the order from latest first. I wanted to make a dark setting much like the modelling software and the shooter genre of themes. Overall, the structuring of the site, including the video on a single page, has been a quick 5 hours in total.

The 'Content' Tab was meant to have a dropdown menu listing all the genres on the page. It was held back with a replacement page until I came to the solution in W3C.

The only things remained in the site was the login, video loading on a seaparate page, and the search results.

The login had taken longer in setting up. The form took a while to fix, but then the log in was more tedious with Javascript involved.
